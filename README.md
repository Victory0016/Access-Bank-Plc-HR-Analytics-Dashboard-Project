# 📝 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing workforce performance, employee engagement, training effectiveness, and HR investment activities at Access Bank Plc using Microsoft Excel.

The analysis was carried out using Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, KPI Cards, Conditional Formatting, Interactive Slicers, and Dashboard Design techniques.

The main objective was to transform raw HR data into meaningful business insights for decision-making.

---

# 🚨 Business Problem

Organizations struggle to monitor workforce productivity, engagement, satisfaction, and training performance effectively.

Without HR analytics:
- Employee dissatisfaction may go unnoticed.
- High-performing and low-performing departments may not be identified quickly.
- Training investments may fail to deliver measurable value.
- Workforce productivity may reduce over time.
- HR decisions may rely on assumptions rather than data.
- Management may struggle to track workforce trends and employee morale.

This project was developed to solve these business challenges using data-driven HR analytics and dashboard reporting.

---

# 🎯 Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this project include:

- Analyze workforce distribution across departments and business units.
- Monitor employee performance and engagement levels.
- Evaluate employee satisfaction and work-life balance.
- Measure training effectiveness and HR investment performance.
- Identify workforce trends and performance patterns.
- Support HR decision-making using data insights.
- Build interactive dashboards for executive reporting.

# ➕ New Calculated Columns

Several additional columns were created to improve the analysis and generate deeper HR insights.

---

## 📌 Age Group

This column was created using an IF statement to categorize employees into different age categories for workforce demographic analysis.

### Categories:
- Young Adults
- Adults
- Elder

---

## 📌 Tenure

This column was created to measure the number of years employees have spent within the organization based on their employment start date.

The tenure analysis helped evaluate employee experience levels and workforce stability.

---

## 📌 Engagement Level

This column was created using engagement score ranges to classify employees into different engagement categories.

### Categories:
- Low Engagement
- Medium Engagement
- High Engagement

This analysis helped identify employees with lower engagement levels that may require HR attention.

---

## 📌 Satisfaction Level

This column was created to categorize employee satisfaction scores into meaningful satisfaction groups.

### Categories:
- Low Satisfaction
- Moderate Satisfaction
- High Satisfaction

This helped evaluate overall employee morale and workplace satisfaction.

---

## 📌 Performance Category

This column was created to group employees based on their performance ratings.

### Categories:
- Low Performer
- Average Performer
- High Performer

The classification made it easier to analyze performance trends across departments and divisions.

---

## 📌 Training Efficiency

This column was created to evaluate the effectiveness of employee training programs by comparing training outcomes and performance indicators.

The analysis helped determine whether training investments were producing positive employee development results.

# 🔍 Key Insights

Some major insights discovered during the analysis include:

- Certain departments recorded lower engagement levels compared to others.
- Employee satisfaction varied significantly across divisions.
- High-performing employees generally showed stronger engagement scores.
- Some training programs produced better performance outcomes than others.
- Workforce distribution was uneven across business units.
- Work-life balance scores influenced employee satisfaction levels.
- Training investments contributed positively to employee development in several departments.

---

# 💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were provided:

- Improve employee engagement programs in low-performing departments.
- Increase focus on employee satisfaction initiatives.
- Strengthen work-life balance policies.
- Monitor employee performance consistently using HR dashboards.
- Optimize training investments toward high-impact programs.
- Use data-driven HR strategies for workforce planning.
- Continuously monitor workforce trends for proactive decision-making.
